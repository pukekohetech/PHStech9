CIRCUIT COACH

Remember

1

LED direction: conventional current enters the anode (+) and leaves the cathode (−).

2

Protect LEDs: use resistance in series with an LED path to limit current.

3

Ohm's law: I = (supply voltage − LED voltage) ÷ resistance.

BOOKLET TARGET

Build the behaviour, not just the picture

Your circuit must do this

  1. Two indicator LEDs remain on while the battery is powered.
  2. A separate warning LED remains off until the wand touches the maze.
  3. Releasing the wand switches the warning LED off again.
  4. LED polarity is correct and current is limited.
Creative freedom: components may be placed anywhere. You can add extra safe LEDs, resistors, switches or parallel branches.
Useful design note: the booklet circuit uses one shared 330 Ω resistor. The simulator accepts that target, but one resistor per parallel LED branch usually gives more predictable current.

Safety ideas

  • Disconnect real power before changing wiring.
  • An LED needs correct polarity.
  • A resistor limits LED current.
  • A battery short can heat wires and the battery.

Useful equations

V = I × R

I = (Vs − Vf) ÷ R

1 A = 1000 mA

Use amps inside the formula.

Keyboard
